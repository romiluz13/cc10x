# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Senior software builders attending a 45-minute keynote. They already use coding agents or understand their potential, but need a rigorous way to design the systems around them.

## Product Purpose

The keynote teaches Harness Engineering and Loop Engineering as a practical field. Its job is to show that reliable agentic engineering is the deliberate design of workflows, context, tools, skills, roles, state, evidence, and feedback loops around an agent. Attendees should leave able to recognize and design a harness for a repeated, high-value workflow in their own environment.

## Brand Personality

Electric, optimistic, open.

The keynote is confident about the future of engineering without pretending that models remove engineering judgment. It feels like an invitation into a powerful discipline: technically serious, emotionally energizing, inspectable, and generous with its methods.

## Anti-references

- Generic AI slop or default SaaS-dashboard visuals
- Decorative cyberpunk, terminal rain, neon-on-black clichés, and fake futurism
- Glassmorphism, gradient text, repeated equal-card grids, and hero-metric templates
- Product tutorials, installation guides, or tool marketing disguised as education
- Empty claims about revolution, magic, 10× gains, or inevitable automation

## Design Principles

1. **Teach the field, not the tool.** CC10x and Auto-Pi are inspectable evidence for a portable discipline, never the product being sold.
2. **Make engineering visible.** Show workflows, evidence, boundaries, state, and decisions as legible systems rather than vague model behavior.
3. **Build confidence through specificity.** Prefer named mechanisms, trade-offs, and proof over category hype.
4. **Keep the future human.** Harnesses make judgment and craft repeatable where appropriate; they do not erase the engineer.
5. **Earn attention visually.** Every visual move should carry meaning, rhythm, or emotion. The deck must feel alive without becoming decoration.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

The keynote prioritizes maximum visual freedom. It will still preserve large display typography and meaningful hierarchy for a conference room, but it is not constrained to a formal WCAG or reduced-motion target.
